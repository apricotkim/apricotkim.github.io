About the group

Physics-based plasma engineering

Our research spans low-temperature to fusion plasmas, applied to plasma source technologies for semiconductor and display processing. By combining experiments, diagnostics, modeling, and simulation, we investigate plasma generation, transport, instability, and control — pursuing world-leading research in the physics and engineering of partially magnetized plasmas.

Research

Integrating experiments & modeling

Four pillars connect fundamental plasma science to semiconductor equipment applications.

SRC

Source engineering

Development and characterization of DC, RF, and microwave plasma sources, gridded ion beam sources, and negative ion sources for processing and beam applications.

DIAG

Plasma diagnostics

Electric, optical, and laser diagnostics — Langmuir probes, LIF, and FFT-based instability characterization with synchronized spatiotemporal measurement.

E×B

Partially magnetized plasmas

Spoke and breathing instabilities, anomalous transport, and ion energization in cross-field devices — the physics that governs modern plasma equipment.

MODEL

Plasma modeling

Instability physics, reduced-theory frameworks, and kinetic simulation validated against experiments to enable predictive equipment design.
